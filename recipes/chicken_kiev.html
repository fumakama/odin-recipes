Chicken Kiev

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 1/2 cups panko breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F.
  2. Using a meat mallet, pound the chicken breasts until they are an even thickness.
  3. In a shallow dish, add the flour. In another shallow dish, beat the eggs. In a third shallow dish, add the panko breadcrumbs.
  4. Season each chicken breast with salt and black pepper. Dip each chicken breast in the flour, then the eggs, and then coat with the panko breadcrumbs.
  5. In a bowl, combine the softened butter, chopped parsley, minced garlic, lemon juice, 1/2 teaspoon salt, and 1/4 teaspoon black pepper.
  6. Place the butter mixture on a sheet of plastic wrap and roll into a log. Chill the butter log in the fridge for 20-30 minutes, or until firm.
  7. Place each chicken breast on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Cut the chilled butter log into 4 equal parts and place one part in the center of each chicken breast.
  8. Fold the chicken breast around the butter, making sure to seal the edges tightly.
  9. Bake the chicken in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the internal temperature of the chicken reaches 165°F.
  10. Let the chicken rest for a few minutes before serving.
